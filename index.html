Self-Assessment Reflection Essay
What I Discovered About Myself
Across all four assessments, a clear and consistent picture emerged: I am someone who leads through people, not over them. My Caretaker score of 9.5 on the DECA inventory, my INFJ personality type, and my Social-dominant CareerZone profile all point to the same core truth — I am most effective and most fulfilled when I am helping others grow.

What surprised me was how much my DECA minor style (Analyzer, 5.9) shows up in my academics — AP Calculus, AP Computer Science, AP Statistics — yet it rarely feels like my natural leadership mode. I lead with heart first, data second. That gap is something I'm actively working to close.
How It Connects to My Career Path
These findings directly validate the career direction I've been building toward. Coaching robotics, teaching coding, and competing in DECA aren't separate pursuits — they are all expressions of the same SCRP Standards: communicating clearly (Standard 2), developing an education and career plan (Standard 3), working productively in teams (Standard 9), and demonstrating creativity and innovation (Standard 10).

Understanding that my natural style is Caretaker — but that epic teams need all four styles — has made me more intentional about when to step into Driver or Analyzer modes. This self-awareness is the foundation of my professional development going forward.

Work Sample — DECA Independent Business Plan
Algi — Sustainable Concrete Additives Business Plan
Integrated Marketing Communications · February 2026
SCRP Standards
Standards 1, 2, 4, 5, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12 — Research · Technical Writing · Teamwork · Sustainability · Innovation

The Algi project was a DECA Independent Business Plan focused on creating sustainable construction materials from invasive Sargassum seaweed. The project addressed environmental issues caused by seaweed blooms while solving durability problems in concrete infrastructure — proposing bio-based additives called AlgiCure and AlgiSeal. It required extensive research on construction materials, environmental sustainability, supply chains, financial projections, and business operations, culminating in a professional written plan and competition presentation.

I applied critical thinking to connect two unrelated problems — concrete cracking and invasive seaweed — into one innovative solution using Microwave-Assisted Extraction (MAE) technology. I contributed to research, financial modeling, customer segmentation, and marketing strategy, using online databases, market reports, and collaborative tools throughout. This project sharpened my ability to translate complex engineering concepts for non-technical audiences and demonstrated my growth in sustainability-focused entrepreneurship, teamwork, and professional communication under competition deadlines.

Work Sample — DECA Innovation Plan
ReInvest — Sustainable Fashion Rental Marketplace
Economics of Business Ownership · February 2025
SCRP Standards
Standards 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 9, 10, 11, 12 — Entrepreneurship · Innovation · Research · Financial Planning · Social Responsibility

ReInvest was a DECA Innovation Plan centered on a sustainable clothing rental marketplace to reduce fast fashion waste and make sustainable fashion more affordable. The platform allowed users to rent clothing from individuals and company-owned inventory via an app and website. The plan addressed textile waste, carbon emissions, labor exploitation, and consumer financial waste — and included customer research, environmental analysis, marketing strategies, AI-powered personalization, operational logistics, and financial projections.

I developed creative customer personas — "Gen-Z Genie," "Flashy Flynn," "Event Andy," and "Donator Don" — to ground the business in real audience needs. I designed a circular economy model where clothing continuously circulates between users instead of being discarded, and incorporated AI personalization features to improve recommendations. Working across research, writing, graphic design, and financial planning taught me how entrepreneurship can directly solve social and environmental issues while remaining profitable — and strengthened my skills in team collaboration, sustainable business modeling, and innovation.
